VR Cameras

Currently Radeon ProRender provides support for 2 types of VR cameras. These include:

  • 360 degree longitudinal panorama or photo-sphere camera.  This provides a rendering of the whole scene mapped onto a sphere and is useful when publishing to sites like YouTube that support 360 degree images or videos.

  • 360 degree longitudinal stereoscopic photo-sphere camera.  This provides a rendering of the scene in two 360 degree images that are stereoscopic placed in an over and under configuration on a single frame suitable for viewing in VR goggles. e.g. see the image below.

To take advantage of the Radeon ProRender VR features it is just a simple matter of placing a physical camera at the preferred location in your scene and changing the render setting so that the preferred output of LatitudeLongitude360 or LatitudeLongitudeStereo is selected from Render Settings -> Camera.

Then render either in Production Rendering Mode or ActiveShade Mode.

Below we have the Vehicle_Booth_Demo scene form the samples provided with the Radeon ProRender download.  Lets reposition one of the camera so we can take advantage of a 360 degree view.

Change the view so you have the top by pressing T.  Select Camera001 right click it and select Move.  Move it so that it is in between the two cars.  As Camera001 is a targeted camera you should see that it will automatically remain pointing to it's target in the middle of the scene.  Remove the pillar/stand from the centre of the scene so we have a better view.

 

Change the view back to Camera001 by right clicking the view and selecting Camera001.

Open the Render Setup dialog and change the Camera drop down to LatitudeLongitude360.

Change any of the others settings to suit your preferences  and desired render.  Press Render to see the result.

Open the Render Setup again and change the Camera drop down to LatitudeLongitudeStereo.
